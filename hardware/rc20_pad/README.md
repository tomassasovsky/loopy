# RC-20-style footswitch pad — master, mould & casting (Route B)

Parametric master for casting replacement rubber pads for the foot pedals
(pedal top 85.9 × 62.8 mm). Generated by [`rc20_pad.py`](rc20_pad.py) (cadquery);
run with the enclosure venv:

```
../enclosure/.venv/bin/python rc20_pad.py
```

Current spec: 58.8 × 81.9 mm footprint (2 mm margin inside the pedal top),
4 mm tall (2 mm base + 2 mm platforms), square corners, flat glue-on bottom.

## The plan: smooth master → silicone mould → cast pads

We do **not** print the mould — printed layer lines would copy into every pad.
Instead: print the **master**, finish it glass-smooth, pour a **silicone mould**
off it, then cast pads in that reusable mould. Because whatever finish the master
has is copied into the mould and then into every pad, **finishing the master is
the whole job** — do it well once.

Outputs in `out/`: `rc20_pad.stl` / `.step` is the master, `rc20_pad_pourbox.*`
is the printable containment box for the mould pour. (`rc20_pad_mould.*` is a
printable negative — only a throwaway test pull, not final pads.)

## 1. Print the master (FDM)

- **PLA**, it primes/sands the best. Solid or high infill so it's rigid enough
  to sand hard.
- **Finest layer height** your printer does cleanly — 0.08–0.12 mm. You'll coat
  and sand regardless, but less to fill = less work.
- **Orient flat-back-down, detail up.** No supports on the detail; the flat back
  (the hidden glue face) gets the bed finish. Turn off/tune elephant-foot
  compensation so the base edge stays crisp; enable **ironing** on top surfaces
  if your slicer has it, for smoother platform tops.

## 2. Finish the master glass-smooth (this is the whole job on FDM)

FDM layer lines *will* copy into the pads unless you kill them here.

1. **Fill the layer lines.** Best: brush a coat of **Smooth-On XTC-3D**
   (self-levelling epoxy) — fills lines in one or two coats and cures rock hard.
   Cheaper alt: several coats of automotive **high-build filler primer**. For any
   deeper valleys, a skim of **glazing/spot putty** (e.g. Bondo) first.
2. **Wet-sand on a hard block**, progressively: 320 → 400 → 600 → 800 → 1200
   (→ 2000 for gloss). The block keeps the platform faces flat and the square
   corners crisp — don't sand freehand or you'll round them. For the 4.5 mm
   grooves and inside corners, fold the paper or use a small flat needle file.
3. **Re-prime and sand again** until no lines remain on any visible face.
4. **Optional gloss:** a coat of clear lacquer + light polish → mirror finish,
   which the silicone replicates exactly.
5. **Seal.** The cured epoxy/primer already seals the porous PLA so silicone
   can't key into it — just make sure it's hard and off-gassed (see warning).

> ⚠️ **Cure inhibition.** Platinum silicone (and platinum pad rubbers) are
> inhibited by *uncured* resin, fresh paint/primer, and sulfur. Let any
> sealer/paint cure hard and off-gas for a few days first, **or** use a
> **tin-cure** mould silicone (OOMOO), which tolerates sealed/resin masters.
> Always spot-test on a scrap before committing the whole pour.

## 3. Make the silicone mould (one-part open block)

The pad is flat-backed with detail on one side and no undercuts, so a simple
open block mould is all you need — its open face gives the pad's flat back.

1. **Containment:** print `rc20_pad_pourbox.stl` (88.8 × 111.9 × 17 mm; 12 mm
   silicone walls + 10 mm cover built in). Its layer lines are on the *outside*
   of the mould, so FDM is fine here. (LEGO / foam board work too.)
2. **Seat the master:** it drops into the 0.6 mm floor recess, flat-back **down**,
   detail up. Tack it with a dab of hot glue so silicone can't creep under the
   back — that keeps the back flat and crisp; it becomes the mould's screed face.
3. **Release:** brush/spray mould release on the master.
4. **Mould silicone:** OOMOO 25 (tin, forgiving) or Mold Star 16 (platinum,
   longer library life, only over a well-cured/sealed master). Mix by weight,
   **vacuum-degas** if you can, and pour a thin stream from one corner so it
   self-levels over the detail and fills the grooves.
5. **Cure & demould:** pull the box and the master → reusable negative.

## 4. Cast the pads

- **Material:** platinum silicone ~Shore A 40–50 (Dragon Skin 30 — tough and
  grippy — or Smooth-Sil 940). Tougher underfoot: polyurethane ~A 60–80
  (PMC-780), but it needs a release and hates moisture. Pigment: Silc-Pig black.
- Release the cavity, mix + degas the rubber, **overfill slightly**, then
  **scrape flush** with the mould's flat top face (= the pad's glue-back).
- Cure, flex the pad out, trim any flash. Reuse the mould for the rest.
- ~15–18 ml of rubber per pad; mix one batch for all pads + ~20 % waste.

At 4 mm, cure shrink is <0.05 mm — ignore it, don't scale the CAD. If a test
pad is too stiff/grippy/thick, change one parameter and regenerate the master.
